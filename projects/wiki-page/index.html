Crown shyness

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

For the album by Trash Boat, see Crown Shyness (album)

Crown shyness (also conopy disengagement[1], conopy shyness[2] or inter-crown spacing[3]) is a phenomenon observed in some tree species , in which the crowns of fully stocked tress do not touch eachother ,forming a conopy with channel-like gaps. [4][5] The phenomenon is most prevalent among trees of the same species,but also occurs between trees of different species. [6][7] There exist many hypotheses as to why crown shyness is an adaptive behavior, and research suggests that it mighy inhibit spread of leaf-eating insect larvea. [8]
