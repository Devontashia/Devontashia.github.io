Pumpkin Pie Biscoff Banana Pudding

This cozy fall dessert puts a seasonal twist on classic banana pudding. Pumpkin puree, warm spices, bananas, and Biscoff cookies come together in creamy layers with drizzles of melted cookie butter. It’s completely no-bake and perfect for making ahead for gatherings or enjoying throughout the week.

Prep Time

45 minutes

Total Time

45 minutes + 6 hours chilling

Yields

12 servings

JUMP TO RECIPE

Ingredients

This recipe uses simple ingredients: Biscoff cookie crumbs and melted butter for the crust, pumpkin puree and instant vanilla pudding mix for the filling, warm spices like pumpkin spice and cinnamon, sweetened condensed milk, softened cream cheese, whipped heavy cream, fresh bananas, and melted Biscoff spread for drizzling between the layers.

Instructions

Blend Biscoff cookies into crumbs and mix with melted butter to form the crust. Press it firmly into your dish. For the filling, whisk together pudding mix, cold milk, and pumpkin puree, then add spices, condensed milk, and cream cheese until smooth. Fold in whipped heavy cream to keep the mixture light and fluffy.

Layer bananas, Biscoff cookies, and the pumpkin pudding mixture over the crust, drizzling melted Biscoff spread between each layer. Chill for at least six hours so the cookies soften and the flavors blend. Before serving, top with extra cookies, banana slices, and leftover crumbs.

Tips

This dessert tastes even better the next day as the cookies soften. For a less sweet version, use slightly less condensed milk. Leftovers keep well for 3 to 4 days in the fridge.
